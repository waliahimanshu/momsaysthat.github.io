What does this phrase mean? I have some unresolved issues with the phrase "To the moon and back". I have seen people using this phrase when they have a splendid time. While, I want to use this phrase when at one moment I am the top of the world and on the other moment right on the floor -witnessing the highs and the lows, the way I did in these first 15 days of February.

February started on a high note. With the much awaited trip to Portugal and Paris, we also marked the first successful and happy year of our marriage. I think my first year of wedding was a piece of cake, with no major fights and no disappointments. Although, I have had my share of roller coaster ride in this year. The major one was being without a job for an year. This was tough, very tough! I haven't ever been without work since the day I completed my graduation. I did not ever think that job hunt would be this difficult here. It definitely hit my self confidence. 

The trip to Paris and Portugal was A.M.A.Z.I.N.G. Both the places are extremely different from each other, in terms of crowd, culture, heritage. While Lisbon was quite, Paris was fast paced. I loved Paris, but Lisbon had my heart for sure. I will visit you again, for sure.
